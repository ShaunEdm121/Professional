Critical Thinking

Each of us has an idea of how the world should work. Some of us may not agree with those particular views. For me, the point of critical thinking is to focus on the few or many grains that make up an idea.

While the concept as a whole may not be viable, the key is to find those morsels of brilliance and incorporate them in to your own.

The next step is to ask the questions that those grains do not answer and keep asking them until the solution becomes clear. If you can include as many people as you can and thus more concepts and ideas, the road to a solution is expedited exponentially.

Leadership

Leadership is not the ability to take command. Leadership is the ability to create and inspire focus.

Rome wasn't built in a day and it most certainly was not built by one person. I believe a true leader is a person that can look at the resources that they have at their disposal and organize those resources for the benefit of achieving a goal.

Much of what I refer to as resources are people. Having the ability to focus many different minds with many different ideas and methods on how things should be done and align them towards a common purpose is the true essence of leadership.

When I am offered the opportunity to take charge, I focus on the people and creating a team. As a guide I show that team how to best utilize their potential and skills for the benefit of everyone.
